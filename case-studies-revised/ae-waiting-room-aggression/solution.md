# Solution: Emergency Department Waiting-Room Aggression

This was solved in the real world.

The UK Design Council and PearsonLloyd created A Better A&E: a package of
patient guidance, department process maps, information panels, staff support
tools, and implementation guidance for NHS emergency departments.

The core move was to make the emergency-department journey legible without
turning clinicians into repeated explainers. Patients could see where they were,
what might happen next, and why waiting occurs. Staff got a matching language and
tools for handling frustrated patients.

Pilot evaluations at Southampton General Hospital and St George's Hospital
reported reduced non-physical aggression and hostility, better patient
experience, improved staff morale, and a positive cost-benefit ratio.
