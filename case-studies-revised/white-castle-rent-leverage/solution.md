# Solution: White Castle Real Estate Leverage

This was solved in the real world.

White Castle made its small restaurant buildings standardized and movable. In
1934 it formed Porcelain Steel Buildings, a subsidiary that produced
prefabricated steel-frame restaurants with porcelain-enamel panels.

The buildings preserved the clean White Castle look while making the restaurant
less permanently attached to one parcel. White Castle Building No. 8 in
Minneapolis was moved after its lease expired in 1950, illustrating the practical
use of that leverage.
