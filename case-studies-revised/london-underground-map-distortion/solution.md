# Solution: London Underground Passenger Map

This was solved in the real world.

Harry Beck designed the 1933 London Underground Tube map as a schematic diagram
rather than a geographically faithful map. He used the logic of circuit diagrams:
colored lines, simplified angles, clear interchange points, and deliberate
compression or expansion of geography where clarity required it.

London Transport initially considered the design too radical, but a trial print
run worked. The map became the model for modern transit diagrams around the
world.
