# Case Study: The Publisher Whose Search Traffic Stopped Arriving

A general-interest publisher enters 2026 with a familiar dashboard problem. The
site still ranks for many evergreen explainers. Google Search Console still shows
impressions. Some stories are still being cited or summarized in search results.
But sessions from classic search are weaker, and the decline is concentrated in
the pages that used to answer ordinary informational queries.

The search page itself has changed. Google AI Overviews and AI Mode, ChatGPT
Search, Perplexity, and similar products can now satisfy a user before the user
visits a publisher's site. Pew Research found in 2025 that Google users clicked a
traditional result in 8% of visits when an AI summary appeared, compared with 15%
when it did not. Clicks on links inside the AI summary were rarer. Google says
its AI features surface links and that no special markup is needed beyond normal
search eligibility, but the measurement is awkward: AI Overviews and AI Mode are
rolled into ordinary Search Console reporting rather than broken out as a clean
distribution channel.

The impact is not even. Industry data from 2026 showed large organic-click drops
for a portfolio of publishers after AI Overviews expanded, while breaking-news
traffic and Discover clicks rose. Other large-scale datasets showed a smaller
aggregate decline across the web, with sharper pain for some publisher classes
than for others. A newsroom can therefore be in real trouble even while someone
else can say search is not dead.

The publisher's internal argument gets messy quickly. The SEO team sees stable
rankings and wants technical fixes. The editorial team wants to protect service
journalism and evergreen explainers because those pages used to pay for less
commercial work. The ad team sees fewer pageviews. The subscription team cares
less about raw visits and more about readers who know the brand well enough to
return directly. The platform team cannot tell whether a citation inside an AI
answer is a substitute for a visit, a brand impression, or just invisible
extraction.

The publisher has to decide what kind of attention it is still earning, what
kind it has lost, and what it should build for next. A recipe database, a local
accountability investigation, a travel guide, a product review, and a breaking
news live blog may not be the same business anymore.
