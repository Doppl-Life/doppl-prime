Problem Recovery

Recover The Ownership Premise

The case is not mainly robotaxi versus Uber. The load-bearing problem is that autonomy removes the human-driver reason for household car ownership, exposing every business model that depends on privately owned idle vehicles.

Hidden constraint
The decisive fork is whether autonomous miles become pure service inventory or whether owned cars become revenue assets in a hybrid fleet.
Falsifier
If households keep buying private autonomous cars at current replacement rates while fleet miles remain marginal, the ownership-unwind thesis weakens.

Residual values, lease math, floorplan lending, and auto ABS become pressure points when fleet utilization reduces household demand for privately owned vehicles.

K2fsd-enforcement-economy

Autonomy changes who is treated as the legal subject of driving: driver, OEM, fleet operator, insurer, or software provider.

K3fsd-mobility-and-time

Dealer floorplan economics depend on inventory velocity and unit sales; both are exposed if mobility reorganizes around fleet miles.
