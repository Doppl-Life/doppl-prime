# Case Study: White Castle Real Estate Leverage

White Castle is expanding a chain of small, standardized hamburger restaurants
in the 1920s and 1930s. The company wants each restaurant to feel clean,
recognizable, and repeatable, not like a one-off roadside lunch counter.

Real estate creates a hostage problem. A good location matters, but buying every
parcel ties up capital and slows expansion. Leasing keeps expansion lighter, but
the restaurant investment becomes vulnerable to the landlord. Once White Castle
has built a recognizable store on a leased parcel, a rent hike or non-renewal can
strand capital that is physically attached to someone else's land.

Harder lease negotiation may help one site. It does not remove the structural
dependence. The company needs the brand consistency of a real building and the
capital discipline of a lease-heavy rollout.

How could White Castle make expansion less vulnerable to any single landlord's
parcel, using materials and operations available at the time?
