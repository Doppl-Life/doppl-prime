# Case Study: Loft Insulation Adoption

UK households are under-adopting loft insulation even when the economics look
good. The offer is easy to defend on paper: less heat loss, lower energy use,
more comfort, and a long-lived home improvement.

The program's problem is not just awareness. Some households express interest.
Some book audits. Some accept that insulation would be sensible. Then the journey
breaks before installation is completed.

That matters because this is not a clean online purchase. It is a domestic
service visit inside a private, awkward part of the house. The household has to
let workers in, prepare the space, deal with disruption, and fit the job into
normal life. A campaign can look persuasive at the leaflet or booking stage and
still fail at the point where the home has to be made ready.

What should the program change if completed installations, not stated interest,
are the real measure?

## Synopsis

UK households under-adopt loft insulation despite favorable economics; interest exists, but the journey breaks down before installation completes.
