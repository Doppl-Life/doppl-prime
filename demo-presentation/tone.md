# Tone

The companion to `[outline.md](./outline.md)`. Everything meta lives here so it never leaks onto the stage. The presentation is played straight; this doc is where we admit what we're doing.

## The register: feather-light wry

**Play it straight, but warm.** It is a real demo of a real system, delivered earnestly — and the satire is a smirk, not a chill. Affectionate, amusing, never dark. The undertone is a knowing wryness the room shares with us, not dread we inflict on it. If a beat starts to bite too hard or curdle, pull it back toward the smile.

The satire is never announced, never explained from the podium. If a line needs the audience to be told it's a joke, the line is wrong. We supply the straight surface; the room supplies the laugh. Our job is to deliver it like we mean every word — because the comedy is in meaning it. Also, the jokes don't always come. Jokes are here and there, not always, and the rest of it has to be explained with the project, which exists in Doppl Prime.  

I'll Say it again and again and again and again. Just a little bit of satire. It's the touch of satire. Just a little bit of joke.

This is not an actual joke. This is actually a real thing, with real people's lives and getting future jobs that depend on it.

## It must stand on its own

No inside references that require knowing us, the team, or the meta. Anyone off the street should be able to watch it as a sincere product demo and leave informed. The second, satirical reading is a free upgrade for the people who catch it — not a prerequisite.

## The undertone (the thing we never say out loud)

Ideas aren't sacred and scarce — they're cheap, a dime a dozen (that's what "could be cheaper" rides on). The venerated, hard part is zero-to-one *success,* the *mindset* behind it, and the work it takes to validate and prove out an Actual Good idea. Doppl industrializes that mindset, generates in the aggregate.

- **Cold open — when the crashes don't come.**
- **The reveal — Doppl on your radar.** The mechanical heart of the cold open: *"this is coming"* = the blip approaching on the radar. **Doppl ≈ Doppler** — the thing you hear before it arrives. The cascade was the Doppler shift; Doppl is the source. Don't overload it with the other puns — one reveal, one pun. "Doppl is..."
- **Outside view.** 
- **Inside view + why it was technically hard.** Dead straight, real engineering, real credibility. This is the load-bearing sincerity that makes the satire land instead of reading as a stunt. Do not undercut it. This one we just need to frame. Somebody else will do the talking on this one.
- **The zero-to-one bit.** The pivot where the satire surfaces — but still played straight. We riff the cult of the precious founding mindset — the sacred "vision" — without naming the person. "They say ideas are cheap." *(beat, to the audience)* "Could be cheaper." Then: we are automating the zero-to-one mindset, stated as a product feature, no irony in the voice.
- **Skin in the game.** The quiet aside. The machine took the work; it politely left you the consequence. Delivered as a perk, not a verdict. This is actually a good idea in which we show that we're thinking, not just generating ideas, but also how to connect to the human world. This part is about the problem recovery stage.
- **Implications & opportunity.** Said with full sincerity and a straight face — which is exactly what makes it land. The one sanctioned break in the deadpan: *"PSaaS… also known as…"* **jazz hands** *"…Pizzazz."* A category name coined and deflated in the same breath. The jazz hands are the punchline; commit to them fully, then snap straight back to earnest. We're inventing a market (the old bespoke-McKinsey one) and naming it something ridiculous on purpose — and we mean it anyway. Part is about the actual Doppl idea stage, and what comes after You have a good idea, And/or realize the unlock.
- **"Anybody got any questions?"** The deferential-assistant reflex ("great question") seeded here as a gesture — finger up, sweep to the back — not yet as a line. It pays off in the close. Plant it light.

## The running gags

Plant, then pay off. Never lampshade.

1. **"Great question." / "Anybody got any questions?"** — seeded as a gesture at the radar reveal. Pays off in the closing line.
2. **"They say ideas are cheap." → "Could be cheaper."** — the turn to the audience is the whole move. Let the second clause land in silence before continuing.
3. **"All you need.........is a great question."** — final line. Rings (1). Lights down on it; do not add anything after.

## The wordplay (don't over-pour)

The Doppl pun vein is generative (radar/Doppler, doppelgänger, double life, "a little Doppl'll do ya"). It's seasoning, not the meal. The radar/Doppler pun is load-bearing — it earns its place by powering the reveal. The rest are garnish: one or two land, more than that tips the whole thing from wry into corny and breaks the straight-faced spell. When in doubt, cut a pun.

## Guardrail

Before any line goes into the presentation: would a stranger understand it, and does it survive without a wink? If it needs the meta to work, it belongs in this file, not on stage.
