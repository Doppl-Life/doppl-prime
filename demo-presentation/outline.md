## 1 · Cold open — When the Crashes Don't Come

Anchor it in the room. It's so interesting to be here in Austin, right now, ground zero for the robotaxi rollout. Punchy and quick. Start with the surface discourse, then go deeper than the pundits do — but go **wide, not deep**. Breadth is the move: the list piles up into weight. No character studies (no Bob-the-billboard-lawyer-and-his-kids); each item is one fast hit. Wry elegy, delivered straight.

- "Interesting to be in Austin." Ground zero for the robotaxi revolution. **Tesla only** — Tesla is the only one with a real shot (not geo-fenced, builds its own cars/cameras, no LiDAR-cost albatross, can drive the cost structure down, controls its own future). Optional one cuttable throwaway dismissing Waymo, then move on fast.
- The surface discourse — what everyone's talking about: cost per mile dropping, crashes down, fewer people hurt.
- The pivot: but they stop too early. Follow it all the way down. What happens When the Crashes Don't Come…?
- The dominoes — **rattled off wide and fast**, fragments, no expansion (also add some real quick stats just in case, easliy dropped):
  - personal-injury lawyers, DUI lawyers, no more cases to to car accidents
  - ERs, trauma units, the nurses and paramedics jobs
  - cops lose the traffic stop — probable cause, the pretext stop, falls away
  - traffic tickets — red-light, speeding — gone; speed-trap towns, municipal budgets
  - truckers (self-driving); the truck stops stop; the towns built around the truck stops
  - auto parts, repair shops, the supply lines — reshuffled or collapsed
  - …et cetera, et cetera, et cetera, find more if you can
- The build: *"This is coming. All this adds up to something. This **means** something."* (emphasis on **means**)
- *"What does this all **mean**?"* — finger up — *"Anybody got any ideas?"* — sweep the finger across the room and land on the projection screen, as I land the punchline.
