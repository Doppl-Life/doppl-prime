# Claims adjustment

Domain memory for how autonomy disintermediates the claims layer — the adjusters, forensic reconstruction, and collision-litigation funnel that crash volume used to feed.

## Load-bearing facts

### Telematics plus OEM direct settlement removes the adjuster intermediary

When the vehicle records the event and the OEM settles directly, the adjuster's job — reconstructing fault from incomplete testimony — disappears. NAIC data already ties telematics adoption to falling claims-processing time, and OEM direct-settlement pilots are live in the Texas and California rollout zones. The claims layer is being disintermediated, not just made efficient.
_Grounded: NAIC telematics/processing-time correlation · OEM direct-settlement pilots (TX, CA)_ ^adjuster-disintermediation

### The collision-litigation funnel dries up at the source

Fewer crashes means fewer injuries means fewer cases: personal-injury firms report declining collision-case intake, and legal-tech capital is rotating out of litigation support into automated dispute resolution. The downstream legal economy contracts from its intake end, where it is hardest to see coming.
